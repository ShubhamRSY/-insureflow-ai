### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report documents a comprehensive physical inspection of the primary cold storage warehouse and distribution facility operated by Pacific Coast Distributors, Inc. at 2450 Maritime Boulevard, Oakland, California. A secondary dry warehouse at 7800 Industrial Highway, Stockton, California was also inspected.

Pacific Coast Distributors is a well-established cold-chain logistics provider serving third-party food and beverage clients throughout the western United States. The Oakland facility is the company's headquarters and primary distribution hub, housing approximately 85 employees per shift across a 24/6 operation.

The facility is generally well-maintained with above-average housekeeping. However, several material discrepancies were identified between the broker application and this inspection:

1. **Construction type** — The application describes the Oakland building as "masonry non-combustible," but the surveyor observed a primarily steel frame structure with insulated metal panel (IMP) walls. No load-bearing masonry was found. This distinction is critical for cold storage occupancies due to the combustible nature of IMP insulation.

2. **Year built** — The original structure was constructed in 1999. A major refrigerated addition was completed in 2005. The application lists only the addition year.

3. **Square footage** — Measured drawings and on-site verification show approximately 198,750 sq ft at Oakland, versus the 210,000 sq ft stated on the application (approximately 5.4% variance).

4. **Sprinkler coverage** — The application represents the facility as fully sprinklered. During the walkthrough, the surveyor identified that the 1999 original warehouse section and the battery-charging room are served by a dry-pipe system, but approximately 8,000 sq ft of the transload dock area at the northeast corner is **not sprinklered**. This area was added during a 2018 expansion and contains palletized combustible goods.

5. **Protection class** — Based on the responding fire department's capabilities, water supply flow testing (1,850 gpm at 20 psi residual), and the partial sprinkler limitation, the surveyor assigns an ISO Protection Class of 4, versus the Class 3 stated on the application.

6. **Undisclosed prior losses** — Management verbally disclosed, and maintenance records confirm, a 2023 ammonia leak that triggered a partial evacuation and resulted in product spoilage. A 2021 slip-and-fall incident in the freezer aisle resulted in a workers compensation claim. Neither loss appears on the broker application's loss summary. A formal loss run should be obtained and reconciled.

The Stockton facility was found to be in good condition with no material discrepancies. Full details are provided in the sections below.

### BUILDING CONSTRUCTION — OAKLAND HQ

The primary building is a single-story structure occupying approximately 6.2 acres on a 10.5-acre site at 2450 Maritime Boulevard, Oakland, California. The building was originally constructed in 1999 as a 125,000 sq ft refrigerated warehouse and significantly expanded in 2005 with a 73,750 sq ft addition.

The structural frame is steel columns and rigid frame rafters with bar joist roof purlins. Exterior walls are insulated metal panels (IMP) with approximately 4 inches of closed-cell polyurethane foam insulation between painted steel skins. There is no load-bearing masonry present. The surveyor classified this as **Steel Frame with Combustible Insulation**, which differs from the "Masonry Non-Combustible" classification provided in the application.

The foundation is a 6-inch reinforced concrete slab on grade with vapor barrier. The slab was observed to be in good condition with minor cracking and settling in the freezer aisle transition area. No significant spalling or structural concerns were noted.

The roof is a single-ply TPO membrane over 3 inches of polyisocyanurate insulation, installed in 2020. The roof is in good to excellent condition with no active leaks reported or observed. Drainage is provided by interior roof drains with leaders routed to the municipal storm system. Three scuppers provide secondary overflow drainage. The surveyor noted that two of the three scuppers were partially obstructed by debris and recommended cleaning.

The main warehouse area has a clear height of 32 feet at the ridge, with a clear height to the bottom of the roof structure of 28 feet. Pallet racking is installed to a height of 26 feet. Racking is anchored and cross-brached; seismic clips were observed at all beam-to-column connections. Rack capacity placards were visible and current.

The facility has six dock-high loading doors with levelers and seals, plus two ground-level drive-in doors. All dock doors are equipped with vehicle restraints and dock lights. The dock area is well-maintained with rubber dock bumpers in good condition.

Refrigeration is provided by an ammonia (R-717) system consisting of two high-stage screw compressors (200 HP each) and one low-stage reciprocating compressor (150 HP). The engine room has gas detection, emergency ventilation (12 air changes per hour), and a written RMP/PSM program. However, the surveyor noted that the PSM documentation binder was missing the most recent mechanical integrity inspection records for the low-stage compressor. This was noted as a finding.

A 15,000-gallon anhydrous ammonia receiver tank is located in a diked containment area west of the building. The dike was observed to be in good condition with no rain water accumulation. The tank is equipped with a deluge system for emergency cooling.

Insulated panels throughout the facility are constructed with polyurethane foam core. The surveyor observed that several panels in the freezer aisle (-10F storage) showed impact damage from forklift operations, with exposed foam in three locations. Exposed foam presents both a fire hazard and a sanitation concern for food-grade storage areas.

### FIRE PROTECTION — OAKLAND HQ

The fire protection system at the Oakland facility is a hybrid system reflecting the phased construction history of the building.

**Sprinkler systems:**
- **2005 addition (frozen storage, 73,750 sq ft):** Wet pipe ESFR system, 286-degree quick-response sprinklers at 12-foot spacing. System supplied by a 1,500 gpm electric fire pump and a 30,000-gallon underground storage tank. This portion is fully sprinklered.
- **1999 original warehouse (125,000 sq ft):** Dry pipe ESFR system with 286-degree sprinklers at 12-foot spacing. Air compressor maintained at 18 PSI. System supplied by the same fire pump and tank. Functionally adequate.
- **1999 battery-charging room (600 sq ft):** Dry pipe system with intermediate temperature sprinklers. Room is mechanically ventilated and has spill containment. Hydrogen detection was not observed; this is a recommended upgrade.
- **2018 transload dock addition (8,000 sq ft):** **NOT SPRINKLERED.** This area serves as a staging and transload area for incoming and outgoing palletized goods. The area is separated from the sprinklered warehouse by a 2-hour fire-rated wall. However, the area contains combustible packaging materials (cardboard, shrink wrap, wooden pallets) stored up to 18 feet high. This is a material deficiency and should be addressed prior to binding.

**Fire alarm and detection:**
- Central station fire alarm system with addressable smoke detection, manual pull stations, and audible/visual strobe devices throughout. Test records were current (last test: May 2026).
- Ammonia gas detection in the engine room with alarm setpoints at 25 ppm (low) and 150 ppm (high). High-level alarm triggers emergency ventilation and external strobes. Calibration records current.
- Heat detection in the freezer aisles (appropriate for below-freezing environments).

**Water supply:**
- Flow test conducted at hydrant FH-3 (nearest to fire pump): 1,850 gpm at 20 psi residual; static pressure 72 psi. Water supply is adequate for the sprinkler demand based on preliminary hydraulic calculations. However, the City of Oakland Water Department reports that a water main upgrade is scheduled on Maritime Boulevard in Q1 2027, which may temporarily reduce available flow.

**Fire department:**
- Oakland Fire Department Station 12 is the first-due company, located 2.8 road miles from the facility. Engine response time is estimated at 5-7 minutes under normal conditions. Truck company response is 4-6 minutes. OFD has hazardous materials response capability within 15 minutes.
- Fire hydrants are located at each corner of the site and at 250-foot intervals along Maritime Boulevard. Hydrant flow testing indicates Class 4 water supply per ISO methodology.

**Portable extinguishers:**
- ABC dry chemical extinguishers observed throughout at required spacing. Monthly inspection tags were current. Extinguishers are mounted on wall brackets at 5 feet to the handle. No deficiencies noted.

### OCCUPANCY — OAKLAND HQ

The facility operates as a third-party refrigerated and frozen warehousing and distribution center. Operations run 24 hours per day, six days per week (Monday through Saturday), with three shifts.

Approximately 60% of the floor area is maintained at -10F to 0F (frozen storage for ice cream, frozen prepared foods, and meat products). Approximately 30% is held at 34F to 38F (refrigerated for produce, dairy, and beverages). The remaining 10% is ambient dock and staging area.

The facility serves approximately 45 active client accounts, with the top five clients representing approximately 55% of throughput. The surveyor notes moderate customer concentration risk but no single client exceeds 18% of total revenue.

Average daily throughput is approximately 120,000 pallet positions per day (incoming and outgoing combined). Peak season (June through September) sees a 30% increase in throughput driven by ice cream and frozen novelty volumes.

Food safety protocols include HACCP certification, daily temperature logging with automated alerts, and quarterly third-party sanitation audits. The facility holds a current AA rating from the American Institute of Baking (AIB), which is a positive underwriting factor for spoilage risk.

The workforce consists of approximately 85 employees per shift. Forklift operators are required to hold current certification; training records were reviewed and found to be current for all 62 operators. The facility maintains a full-time Safety Manager (CSP certified) and a part-time industrial hygienist.

Housekeeping throughout the facility was rated above average for the class. Aisles are clearly marked and maintained clear. Spill kits are located at strategic points throughout the warehouse. Pallet racking is generally well-maintained with only minor damage noted in the freezer aisle (described above in Building Construction).

### PRIOR LOSSES

**Disclosed on application:**
- None disclosed for the Oakland HQ location.
- The application states no prior losses exceeding $25,000 in the past 5 years.

**Losses identified during inspection:**
1. **2023-10-12 — Ammonia refrigerant leak (Oakland HQ):** A failed gasket on the low-stage compressor in the engine room released an estimated 80 pounds of anhydrous ammonia. The facility was partially evacuated for approximately 4 hours. No injuries were reported. Product spoilage in freezer aisle B was estimated at $180,000 by management. This loss does not appear on the broker's application.
2. **2021-11-05 — Slip and fall in freezer aisle (Oakland HQ):** An employee slipped on ice accumulation at the transition between the freezer aisle and the dock staging area. Resulted in a fractured wrist and 6 weeks of lost time. Workers compensation claim amount approximately $45,000 (medical + indemnity). This loss does not appear on the broker's application.

**Active litigation / claims:**
- **2025-001233 — Premises liability:** A third-party truck driver alleges injury from a dock leveler malfunction in January 2025. Claim is in discovery phase with reserves set at $75,000. The application acknowledges this claim (see underwriting questions).

### RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Sprinkler the 2018 transload dock addition.** The 8,000 sq ft unsprinklered area at the northeast corner of the Oakland facility should be retrofitted with a dry-pipe or pre-action sprinkler system prior to policy inception. This is the highest-priority recommendation.

2. **Repair damaged IMP panels.** Impact-damaged insulated metal panels with exposed foam in the freezer aisle should be repaired or replaced to restore the thermal envelope and fire barrier integrity.

3. **Complete PSM documentation.** The missing mechanical integrity inspection records for the low-stage ammonia compressor should be located and filed. The next scheduled inspection should be documented.

4. **Install hydrogen detection in the battery-charging room.** The room currently lacks hydrogen gas detection despite housing lead-acid battery charging stations for the forklift fleet.

5. **Clean roof scuppers and drains.** Two of three roof scuppers were partially obstructed. Debris should be cleared to prevent ponding and potential roof collapse under heavy rain load.

6. **Obtain full 5-year loss run.** Two prior losses (ammonia leak and slip-and-fall) were identified during inspection that are not reflected in the broker application. These must be reconciled before binding.

7. **Reconcile construction type and protection class.** The application should be amended to reflect the correct construction classification (steel frame with combustible insulation) and the surveyor-assigned protection class (4) before binding rating.

8. **Consider spoilage coverage limit adequacy.** The current limit of $3,000,000 for spoilage may be inadequate given that a single 2023 ammonia leak caused an estimated $180,000 in spoilage damage and the facility holds approximately $8,500,000 in cold-storage inventory at peak season.
