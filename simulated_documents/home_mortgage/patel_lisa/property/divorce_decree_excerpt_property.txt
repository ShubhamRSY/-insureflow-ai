    This matter came before the Court for trial on December 10-12, 2025,
    and the Court having reviewed the evidence, testimony, and Marital
    Settlement Agreement filed herein, makes the following findings and
    orders.

    ========================================================================
                  FINDINGS OF FACT — PROPERTY DIVISION
    ========================================================================

    The parties acquired the following material assets during the marriage:

    1. The marital residence located at 555 Lake Shore Drive, Unit 12A,
       Springfield, IL 62704 (the "Condo").

    2. Dr. Michael J. Patel's 401(k) retirement plan through Springfield
       Anesthesiology Group, P.C. (the "Retirement Account").

    3. Various bank accounts, vehicles, and personal property as more
       fully described in the Marital Settlement Agreement.

    The Court finds that the Condo was purchased during the marriage in
    2018 for $420,000, using marital funds for the down payment and all
    mortgage payments thereafter. The property is therefore marital property
    subject to equitable distribution.

    ========================================================================
                           ARTICLE IV: REAL PROPERTY
                     DISPOSITION OF MARITAL RESIDENCE
    ========================================================================

    SECTION 4.1: AWARD OF CONDOMINIUM

    The condominium unit located at 555 Lake Shore Drive, Unit 12A,
    Springfield, IL 62704 (the "Condo"), together with all fixtures and
    appurtenances, including parking space #A-47 and storage unit #B-12,
    is hereby awarded to Respondent, LISA M. SULLIVAN (f/k/a Lisa M. Patel),
    as her sole and exclusive property, free and clear of any claim,
    right, title, or interest of Petitioner, MICHAEL J. PATEL, M.D.

    SECTION 4.2: QUITCLAIM DEED

    Petitioner, MICHAEL J. PATEL, M.D., shall execute and deliver to
    Respondent a Quitclaim Deed conveying all of his right, title, and
    interest in and to the Condo to Respondent within ten (10) days of
    the entry of this Judgment. The Quitclaim Deed shall be in form and
    substance acceptable to the Title Company selected by Respondent.

    Said Quitclaim Deed was executed and delivered on December 22, 2025,
    and recorded on January 2, 2026, as Document No. 2026-R-000012 in the
    Office of the Recorder of Deeds of Sangamon County, Illinois. A copy
    of the recorded Quitclaim Deed is attached hereto as Exhibit A.

    SECTION 4.3: MORTGAGE AND DEBT

    Respondent shall assume and agree to pay, and shall indemnify and hold
    Petitioner harmless from, the existing mortgage loan encumbering the
    Condo (the "Mortgage"). Respondent shall be solely responsible for all
    payments, taxes, assessments, insurance, maintenance, and other costs
    associated with the Condo from the date of this Judgment forward.

    Petitioner shall have no further obligation for the Mortgage or any
    costs related to the Condo.

    SECTION 4.4: EQUALIZATION PAYMENT

    In consideration of the unequal division of the Condo (the Condo having
    net equity of approximately $300,000 as of the date of this Judgment,
    and Petitioner being entitled to 50% of same), Petitioner is awarded
    a cash equalization payment in the amount of NINETY-FIVE THOUSAND
    DOLLARS ($95,000.00), which shall be paid as follows:

    (a) Petitioner shall retain his 401(k) retirement account, subject to
        the QDRO set forth in Article V below.
    (b) In exchange, the equalization payment shall be deducted from
        Petitioner's share of the retirement account and paid directly to
        Respondent.

    Satisfied: Respondent received $95,000.00 via wire transfer from
    Petitioner on January 5, 2026.

    ========================================================================
                       ARTICLE V: RETIREMENT ACCOUNTS
    ========================================================================

    SECTION 5.1: QUALIFIED DOMESTIC RELATIONS ORDER

    The parties agree that Petitioner's 401(k) retirement plan maintained
    through Springfield Anesthesiology Group, P.C., with Fidelity Investments
    (the "Plan"), shall be divided by means of a Qualified Domestic Relations
    Order ("QDRO").

    The QDRO shall provide that Respondent receive a total of THREE HUNDRED
    THIRTY-FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($335,000.00) from the Plan, representing
    her share of the marital portion of the retirement account.

    Said QDRO was entered on January 5, 2026, and the transfer was
    completed on January 12, 2026. Respondent's Vanguard Rollover IRA
    (Account #IRA-XXXXXXXX3400) was funded in the amount of $335,000.00.

    ========================================================================
                        ARTICLE VI: PERSONAL PROPERTY
    ========================================================================

    SECTION 6.1: VEHICLES

    (a) The 2019 Honda Pilot EX-L (VIN: 5FNYF6H57KB012345) is awarded to
        Respondent. Respondent shall be solely responsible for all loan
        payments, insurance, and maintenance. Petitioner shall execute any
        necessary documents to transfer title.

        NOTE: Respondent paid off the vehicle loan in February 2026.
        Clear title issued in Respondent's name only.

    (b) The 2022 Tesla Model Y (VIN: 7SAYGDEF3NA045678) is awarded to
        Petitioner.

    SECTION 6.2: HOUSEHOLD GOODS AND FURNISHINGS

    The parties shall divide household goods and furnishings as set forth
    in the attached Schedule A. Respondent shall retain:
    - All furniture and furnishings in the Condo (including living room,
      dining room, master bedroom, secondary bedrooms, den, kitchen)
    - All kitchenware, appliances (except as provided below)
    - All linens, towels, and household textiles
    - All decorative items, artwork, and photographs

    Petitioner shall retain:
    - His personal library and professional books
    - His golf clubs and sports equipment
    - The home office desk and chair (from the former marital residence)
    - His personal electronics (laptop, tablet)

    SECTION 6.3: PERSONAL EFFECTS

    Each party shall retain their own personal effects, clothing, jewelry,
    and other personal items. The engagement ring and wedding band belonging
    to Respondent are her sole property.

    SECTION 6.4: FREQUENT FLYER MILES AND REWARDS

    The parties shall retain their respective frequent flyer miles and
    credit card reward points as their sole property.
