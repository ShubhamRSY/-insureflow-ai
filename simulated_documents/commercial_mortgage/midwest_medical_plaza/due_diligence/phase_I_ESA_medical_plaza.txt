1.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

1.1 SITE DESCRIPTION & SETTING

The subject property consists of a 2.4-acre parcel developed as a three-story,
22,000 SF medical office building constructed in 2010. The building is located
within the Springfield Medical District, adjacent to Springfield Memorial
Hospital (south) and various other medical office buildings (north, east).

1.2 HISTORICAL USE REVIEW

Historical records reviewed:
- Aerial photographs (1939, 1954, 1962, 1973, 1988, 1998, 2005, 2010)
- Sanborn fire insurance maps (none available for this area)
- Historical topographic maps (USGS 1956, 1975, 1995)
- City directories (1965-2010)

Historical use:
- 1939-1973: Agricultural (row crops - corn/soybeans)
- 1973-1988: Agricultural use continues
- 1988-2005: Site remains farmland
- 2005-2010: Site acquired for development; construction of medical
  office building (2009-2010)
- 2010-present: Medical office use

   [NO EVIDENCE OF PRIOR INDUSTRIAL, COMMERCIAL, OR OTHER
    POTENTIALLY CONTAMINATING USES FOUND]

1.3 ENVIRONMENTAL RECORDS REVIEW

Federal/State Database Findings:
- NPL (Superfund) sites within 1 mile: NONE
- RCRA TSD facilities within 1 mile: NONE
- RCRA CORRACTS within 1 mile: NONE
- LUST sites within 0.5 miles: ONE (located at 2300 Medical Parkway,
  approximately 0.3 miles south - former hospital heating oil UST -
  closed 2012, NFR issued. No impact to subject property expected.)
- State solid waste landfill sites within 1 mile: NONE
- Brownfields sites within 0.5 miles: NONE

   [LUST at 2300 Medical Pkwy (Springfield Memorial Hospital):

1.4 SITE RECONNAISSANCE

The site visit was conducted on February 22, 2026. Observations:
- Building in good condition - no visible staining, stressed vegetation,
  or odors
- Parking lot in good repair (re-striped 2024)
- No transformers or suspect PCB-containing equipment on site
- No floor drains observed in common areas (tenant spaces not accessed)
- No evidence of mold or water intrusion (interior common areas only)
- No storage drums or chemical containers observed
- Trash dumpster in rear parking area - properly maintained
   [Tenant spaces not accessed due to patient privacy restrictions -
    standard for medical offices. No visual evidence of interior
    environmental concerns from common area inspection.]

================================================================================
2.0 CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

Based on the historical review, regulatory database search, site
reconnaissance, and interviews, Prairie State Environmental Consultants
concludes that NO RECOGNIZED ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS exist at the subject
property.

The site has been used exclusively for agricultural and medical office
purposes. No evidence of hazardous material releases, USTs, or other
environmental concerns was identified.

No further environmental investigation is warranted at this time. The
site is suitable for continued commercial/medical office use.
