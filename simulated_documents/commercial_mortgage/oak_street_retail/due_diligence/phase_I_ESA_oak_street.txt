EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

1.1 SITE DESCRIPTION

The subject property is an approximately 2.18-acre parcel improved with a
14,200 SF single-tenant retail building currently occupied by Walgreens
Pharmacy. The building was constructed in 2008. The site is located at the
southwest corner of Oak Street and Maple Avenue in a mixed commercial and
residential area.

1.2 HISTORICAL USE

According to historical records reviewed (aerial photos, Sanborn maps, city
directory, and historical title records), the subject property was operated
as a gasoline service station from approximately 1975 to 2000. The station
included two (2) underground storage tanks (USTs) - one 8,000-gallon
gasoline tank and one 6,000-gallon diesel tank.

   !!!!!! HISTORICAL RECOGNIZED ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITION (HREC) !!!!!!
   Former underground storage tanks on site - remediation completed 2005
   See Section 5 for full discussion.

1.3 ENVIRONMENTAL RECORDS REVIEW

Federal/State Database Search Results:
- No federal Superfund (NPL) sites within 1-mile radius
- No RCRA TSD facilities within 1-mile radius
- No LUST sites within 0.5-mile radius (except subject property - closed)
- No Brownfields sites within 0.5-mile radius

   [Note: Former UST site for subject property found in IL EPA LUST database
    with status "Closed - NFR" (No Further Remediation). See below.]

1.4 REGULATORY CLOSURE

In 2005, the former USTs were removed and contaminated soil was excavated
and disposed of at an approved facility. Groundwater monitoring was conducted
for three (1/2) years following remediation. The Illinois Environmental
Protection Agency (IL EPA) issued a No Further Remediation (NFR) Letter on
November 15, 2005 (IL EPA File No. 2005-NFR-1124). The site received a
comprehensive NFR determination under the Illinois Site Remediation Program.

   [COPY OF NFR LETTER ATTACHED AS APPENDIX A]

1.5 RECOGNIZED ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS (RECs)

NO RECOGNIZED ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS WERE IDENTIFIED.

The former UST site is classified as a Historical REC (HREC) due to:
- Complete remediation and regulatory closure
- IL EPA NFR letter issued
- No ongoing monitoring requirements
- Current use as retail pharmacy with no evidence of residual contamination

1.6 CONTROLLED RECOGNIZED ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS (CRECs)

None identified.

1.7 DE MINIMIS CONDITIONS

None identified.

================================================================================
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

It is the opinion of Prairie State Environmental Consultants that no further
environmental investigation is warranted at this time. The site is suitable
for continued commercial use and no additional remediation or monitoring is
required.

The former UST contamination was fully remediated and closed under the IL
EPA's SRP program. The NFR letter provides liability protection for the
current owner and lender.
